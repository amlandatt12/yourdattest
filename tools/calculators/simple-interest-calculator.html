About Simple Interest Calculator

Simple interest is a fundamental concept in finance that calculates the interest earned or paid on a principal amount over a specified period at a fixed interest rate. Unlike compound interest, simple interest is not compounded, meaning it doesn't earn interest on previously earned interest.

Our Simple Interest Calculator is a free online tool designed to help individuals, students, and professionals calculate simple interest quickly and accurately. How Simple Interest Works

The formula for calculating simple interest is straightforward:

Simple Interest = (Principal √ó Rate √ó Time) / 100

Where:

  • Principal (P) is the initial amount of money
  • Rate (R) is the annual interest rate (in percentage)
  • Time (T) is the time period in years

For example, if you invest $1,000 at 5% interest for 3 years, the simple interest would be:

Simple Interest = (1000 √ó 5 √ó 3) / 100 = $150

Total Amount = Principal + Interest = $1,000 + $150 = $1,150

When to Use Simple Interest

Simple interest is commonly used for:

  • Short-term loans
  • Personal loans
  • Certificates of deposit
  • Treasury bills
  • Some types of bonds
  • Educational loans

It's less common for long-term investments where compound interest is typically used.

Difference Between Simple and Compound Interest

While simple interest is calculated only on the principal amount, compound interest is calculated on the principal plus any previously earned interest. This means compound interest grows faster over time, especially for longer periods.

For instance, $1,000 at 5% simple interest for 3 years = $150 interest

The same amount at 5% compound interest annually = $157.63 interest

The difference becomes more significant over longer time periods.
