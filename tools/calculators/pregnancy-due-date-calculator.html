About Pregnancy Due Date Calculator

Our free pregnancy due date calculator helps expecting parents estimate when their baby might arrive. Based on the widely-used Naegele's rule, this calculator provides an estimated due date by adding 280 days (40 weeks) to the first day of your last menstrual period. While this method gives a good approximation, remember that only about 5% of babies are born exactly on their due date.

Understanding Due Date Calculations

The most common method for calculating due dates is Naegele's rule, developed by German obstetrician Franz Naegele in 1812. This rule assumes a typical pregnancy lasts 266 days from conception, but since conception usually occurs about 14 days after the first day of the last menstrual period, the total becomes approximately 280 days from LMP.

  • Standard pregnancy length: 40 weeks from LMP
  • From conception: Approximately 38 weeks
  • Full term: 37-42 weeks from LMP
  • Preterm: Before 37 weeks
  • Post-term: After 42 weeks

Factors Affecting Due Dates

Several factors can influence when your baby actually arrives:

  • Maternal age: Older mothers may deliver earlier
  • Previous pregnancies: Second-time mothers often deliver earlier
  • Baby's gender: Boy babies tend to arrive later
  • Multiple pregnancies: Twins usually arrive earlier
  • Maternal health: Certain conditions can affect timing
  • Genetic factors: Family history plays a role

When to See a Healthcare Provider

While our calculator provides a good estimate, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional for accurate pregnancy dating. Ultrasound measurements taken between 8-12 weeks are the most accurate method for determining gestational age. Always seek medical advice for:

  • Confirming your pregnancy
  • Establishing an accurate due date
  • Monitoring fetal development
  • Planning prenatal care

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the due date calculation? +

The calculator uses Naegele's rule, which is accurate for about 80% of pregnancies. However, only 5% of babies are born exactly on their due date. Actual delivery can occur anywhere from 37-42 weeks.

What is the LMP and why is it important? +

LMP stands for Last Menstrual Period - the first day of your last period before pregnancy. It's used as the starting point for due date calculations because it's usually easier to remember than conception date.

Should I use this instead of seeing a doctor? +

No, this calculator provides only an estimate. Always consult your healthcare provider for accurate due date determination, especially if you have irregular periods or other complicating factors.

What if I know my conception date instead? +

If you know your exact conception date, add approximately 266 days for a more accurate estimate. However, LMP-based calculations are more commonly used by healthcare providers.

What factors can change my actual due date? +

Maternal age, previous pregnancies, baby's gender, multiple pregnancies, and maternal health conditions can all influence when you actually deliver. Your doctor may adjust your due date based on ultrasound measurements.
