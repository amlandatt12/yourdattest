What is LCM (Least Common Multiple)?

The Least Common Multiple (LCM) of two or more integers is the smallest positive integer that is divisible by each of the numbers. In simpler terms, it's the smallest number that all your input numbers can divide into evenly without leaving a remainder.

For example, consider the numbers 4 and 6. The multiples of 4 are: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24... The multiples of 6 are: 6, 12, 18, 24, 30... The common multiples are 12, 24, 36, and so on. The smallest of these common multiples is 12, so LCM(4, 6) = 12.

What is GCD (Greatest Common Divisor)?

The Greatest Common Divisor (GCD), also called the Greatest Common Factor (GCF) or Highest Common Factor (HCF), is the largest positive integer that divides each of the numbers without leaving a remainder. It's the biggest number that is a factor of all your input numbers.

For example, with numbers 12 and 18: The factors of 12 are: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12. The factors of 18 are: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18. The common factors are 1, 2, 3, and 6. The greatest of these is 6, so GCD(12, 18) = 6.

Calculation Methods

Finding GCD - The Euclidean Algorithm: Our calculator uses the efficient Euclidean algorithm to find the GCD. This ancient algorithm (dating back to around 300 BC) repeatedly divides the larger number by the smaller and takes the remainder, replacing the larger with the smaller and the smaller with the remainder, until the remainder is zero. The last non-zero remainder is the GCD.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between LCM and GCD? +

LCM (Least Common Multiple) is the smallest positive number that is divisible by all given numbers - it's the smallest number that contains all your numbers as factors. GCD (Greatest Common Divisor), also known as HCF, is the largest positive number that divides all given numbers without remainder - it's the biggest number that is a factor of all your numbers. For example, for 12 and 18: LCM is 36 (smallest number both divide into evenly) and GCD is 6 (largest number that divides both evenly). They're opposite concepts: LCM is about multiples, GCD is about divisors.

How do you calculate LCM of multiple numbers? +

To calculate LCM of multiple numbers, you can use two main methods: 1) Prime Factorization Method: Find prime factorization of each number, then for each prime factor, take the highest power that appears in any factorization, and multiply these together. 2) Successive Method: Find LCM of the first two numbers using the formula LCM(a,b) = (a Ã— b) / GCD(a,b), then find LCM of that result with the third number, continue until all numbers are processed. Our calculator uses the efficient successive method with the Euclidean algorithm for GCD calculation.

What is the Euclidean algorithm for finding GCD? +

The Euclidean algorithm is an efficient method to find GCD that dates back to ancient Greece (around 300 BC). Here's how it works: Given two numbers a and b (where a â‰¥ b), divide a by b and find the remainder r. Then replace a with b and b with r, and repeat the process. Continue until the remainder is 0. The last non-zero remainder is the GCD. Example for GCD(48, 18): 48 = 18Ã—2 + 12, then 18 = 12Ã—1 + 6, then 12 = 6Ã—2 + 0. The last non-zero remainder is 6, so GCD(48, 18) = 6. Can LCM and GCD be calculated for more than two numbers? +

Yes, absolutely! Both LCM and GCD can be calculated for any quantity of numbers. For GCD, the property is associative: GCD(a, b, c) = GCD(GCD(a, b), c). You find GCD of the first two numbers, then find GCD of that result with the third number, and continue. The same principle applies to LCM: LCM(a, b, c) = LCM(LCM(a, b), c). Our calculator handles this automatically for any quantity of numbers you enter. For example, for numbers 12, 18, and 24: GCD(12,18) = 6, then GCD(6,24) = 6, so the overall GCD is 6. LCM(12,18) = 36, then LCM(36,24) = 72, so the overall LCM is 72.
