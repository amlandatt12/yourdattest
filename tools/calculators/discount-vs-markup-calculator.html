What is Discount vs Markup?

Discount and markup are two fundamental pricing concepts that represent opposite approaches to pricing strategy:

Discount

A reduction from the original price. When you apply a discount, you're reducing the selling price to attract customers or clear inventory. The discount amount represents money you're giving up to make the sale.

Markup

An increase added to the cost price. Markup represents the profit margin you add to cover costs and generate profit. It's the amount above cost that you charge customers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between discount and markup? +

Discount reduces the selling price from the original amount (e.g., $100 item at 20% discount sells for $80). Markup increases the cost price to determine the selling price (e.g., $80 cost with 25% markup sells for $100). Discount is customer-focused pricing, while markup is profit-focused pricing.

Profit margin is calculated as: (Selling Price - Cost Price) √∑ Selling Price √ó 100. For example, if you buy at $80 and sell at $100, your profit margin is ($100-$80)√∑$100√ó100 = 20%. Note that markup percentage is different: ($100-$80)√∑$80√ó100 = 25%. Our calculator shows both values for complete analysis.

What percentage markup should I use for my products? +

Markup percentages vary by industry: retail typically uses 50-100%, restaurants 200-300%, electronics 20-50%, clothing 100-200%. Consider your costs (materials, labor, overhead), competition, and desired profit. Use our calculator to test different scenarios and find the optimal markup for your business.

How do discounts affect my profit margins? +

Discounts directly reduce your profit margins. For example, a 20% discount on a $100 item (selling for $80) reduces profit from $20 to $0 if your cost was $80. Use our calculator to see exactly how discounts impact your bottom line and plan promotions that maintain profitability while increasing sales volume.
